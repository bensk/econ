Aim: How can we conduct a Socratic Seminar?

Do Now: Take a seat with your partner. Open your shared Socratic Seminar Worksheet check your email!


Before we begin...

  1. Set your goals with your partner.
  2. Coaches, sit behind your partner on the outside of the circle.
  3. If you are discussing, begin looking through the questions for one you want to pose.

Goals

Coaches: You will be assessing your partner.
  • Cited reasons and evidence for statements
  • Provided relevant and insightful comments
  • Spoke loudly and clearly
  • Stayed focused on the conversation
  • Invited others to the discussion
  • Shared air time equally with others
  • Moved the discussion to a deeper level

Questions

  • Why are prices different around the world?
  • Do the patterns of supply and demand apply to all products?
  • Why is entrepreneurship essential to brining together the factors of production?
  • Is quality more important than quantity
  • Is anything really "free" in this world?
  • Why do people take risks?
  • What does the statement mean “there ain't no such thing as free lunch” mean?
  • How was the economy created?
  • Students get free lunch now. Where does the rest of the money go?
  • How does getting free lunch affect the government or economy?
  • Why does the sneaker cost more when the materials are cheaper?
  • Why isn’t Economics just about money?
  • Why are people desperate for oil? Why is oil so important for the economy
  • Why isn’t the OPEC cartel controlling the oil distribution with an iron fist like they have in the past?
  • If oil spills are so dangerous and dirty, why do we still drill?
  • What do sneakers and oil have in common?
  • Why do we use graphs to portray supply and demand?
  • Why would Nike intentionally create a secondary market?
  • How do companies come up with their prices?
  • How does race factor into the economy?
